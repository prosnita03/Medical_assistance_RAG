=== CARDIOVASCULAR DISEASES ===

HYPERTENSION (High Blood Pressure)
Definition: A chronic condition where blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated (≥130/80 mmHg per ACC/AHA 2017 guidelines).

Symptoms:
- Most people have no symptoms (often called the "silent killer")
- Severe hypertension may cause: severe headache, fatigue, confusion, vision problems
- Chest pain, difficulty breathing, irregular heartbeat
- Blood in urine, pounding in chest/neck/ears

Risk Factors:
- Family history, age (over 55 for men, 65 for women), race (higher in Black adults)
- Being overweight or obese
- Physical inactivity, tobacco use, too much salt, low potassium
- Drinking too much alcohol, stress, certain chronic conditions

Diagnosis: Blood pressure measurement (two or more readings on separate occasions)

Treatment:
- Lifestyle changes: DASH diet, regular exercise, weight loss, limiting alcohol and sodium
- Medications: ACE inhibitors (lisinopril), ARBs (losartan), calcium channel blockers (amlodipine), thiazide diuretics

Complications: Heart attack, stroke, heart failure, kidney disease, vision loss

---

CORONARY ARTERY DISEASE (CAD)
Definition: Narrowing or blockage of coronary arteries due to plaque buildup (atherosclerosis).

Symptoms:
- Chest pain (angina): pressure, squeezing, or tightness, often triggered by exertion
- Shortness of breath
- Heart attack symptoms: crushing chest pain, pain radiating to arm/jaw/neck, sweating, nausea
- Fatigue, weakness

Risk Factors: Hypertension, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, obesity, family history, age

Diagnosis: EKG, stress test, coronary angiography, CT scan

Treatment:
- Medications: Aspirin, statins, beta-blockers, nitrates
- Procedures: Angioplasty and stent placement, coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG)
- Lifestyle: Quit smoking, heart-healthy diet, exercise

---

HEART FAILURE
Definition: The heart cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs.

Symptoms:
- Shortness of breath during activity or when lying down
- Persistent cough or wheezing with white or pink blood-tinged phlegm
- Swelling (edema) in legs, ankles, feet
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat
- Fatigue and weakness
- Sudden weight gain from fluid retention
- Lack of appetite and nausea

Types: Systolic (reduced ejection fraction), Diastolic (preserved ejection fraction), Right-sided, Left-sided

Treatment:
- Medications: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, beta-blockers, diuretics (furosemide), aldosterone antagonists
- Devices: Pacemakers, ICDs, cardiac resynchronization therapy
- Surgery: Heart transplant in severe cases

=== DIABETES MELLITUS ===

TYPE 2 DIABETES
Definition: A metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar, insulin resistance, and relative insulin deficiency.

Symptoms:
- Increased thirst (polydipsia) and frequent urination (polyuria)
- Increased hunger (polyphagia)
- Unintended weight loss
- Fatigue and lack of energy
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing sores or frequent infections
- Darkened skin in body creases (acanthosis nigricans)
- Tingling, numbness, or pain in hands or feet

Diagnostic Criteria:
- Fasting blood glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
- 2-hour glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL during OGTT
- HbA1c ≥ 6.5%
- Random glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL with symptoms

Risk Factors:
- Overweight or obese (BMI ≥ 25)
- Prediabetes, family history, age ≥ 45
- Physical inactivity, gestational diabetes history
- Polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS)

Treatment:
- Lifestyle: Weight loss (5-10% body weight reduces risk significantly), physical activity (150 min/week), healthy diet
- First-line medication: Metformin
- Additional medications: SGLT2 inhibitors, GLP-1 receptor agonists, DPP-4 inhibitors, sulfonylureas, insulin
- Monitoring: Regular HbA1c testing (target <7%), blood pressure, lipids, kidney function

Complications:
- Diabetic neuropathy (nerve damage)
- Diabetic nephropathy (kidney disease)
- Diabetic retinopathy (eye disease)
- Cardiovascular disease
- Diabetic foot complications

TYPE 1 DIABETES
Definition: Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells leading to absolute insulin deficiency.

Symptoms: Similar to Type 2 but often more sudden onset; may present with diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)

DKA Symptoms: Excessive thirst, frequent urination, nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain, fruity breath, confusion

Treatment: Lifelong insulin therapy (basal-bolus regimen), blood glucose monitoring, carbohydrate counting

=== RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS ===

ASTHMA
Definition: Chronic inflammatory disease of the airways causing recurrent episodes of wheezing, breathlessness, chest tightness, and coughing.

Symptoms:
- Wheezing (whistling sound when breathing)
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness or pain
- Cough, especially at night or early morning
- Symptoms triggered by: exercise, allergens, cold air, stress, respiratory infections

Severity Classification: Intermittent, Mild Persistent, Moderate Persistent, Severe Persistent

Treatment:
- Short-acting bronchodilators (SABAs): Albuterol (salbutamol) — for quick relief
- Inhaled corticosteroids (ICS): Fluticasone, budesonide — controller therapy
- Long-acting bronchodilators (LABAs): Salmeterol — add-on therapy
- Leukotriene modifiers: Montelukast
- Biologic therapies for severe asthma: Omalizumab, mepolizumab

Triggers to Avoid: Allergens (dust mites, pet dander, pollen), tobacco smoke, air pollution, strong odors

---

CHRONIC OBSTRUCTIVE PULMONARY DISEASE (COPD)
Definition: Progressive lung disease including emphysema and chronic bronchitis, characterized by airflow limitation.

Symptoms:
- Chronic cough with mucus production (chronic bronchitis)
- Shortness of breath, especially during activities
- Wheezing, chest tightness
- Frequent respiratory infections
- Fatigue, unintended weight loss (in severe cases)
- Cyanosis (bluish discoloration of lips or fingernails)

Causes: Cigarette smoking (most common), occupational dust/chemicals, air pollution, alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency

Diagnosis: Spirometry (FEV1/FVC ratio < 0.7), chest X-ray, CT scan

GOLD Staging:
- Stage I (Mild): FEV1 ≥ 80% predicted
- Stage II (Moderate): 50% ≤ FEV1 < 80%
- Stage III (Severe): 30% ≤ FEV1 < 50%
- Stage IV (Very Severe): FEV1 < 30%

Treatment:
- Smoking cessation (most important intervention)
- Bronchodilators: SABAs, SAMAs (ipratropium), LABAs, LAMAs (tiotropium)
- Inhaled corticosteroids for frequent exacerbations
- Pulmonary rehabilitation
- Supplemental oxygen if SpO2 < 88%
- Vaccinations: Influenza, pneumococcal

=== NEUROLOGICAL CONDITIONS ===

MIGRAINE
Definition: A recurrent headache disorder manifesting in attacks lasting 4-72 hours, typically unilateral, pulsating, moderate to severe intensity.

Phases: Prodrome, Aura (in ~25%), Headache, Postdrome

Symptoms:
- Severe, throbbing or pulsating headache (often one-sided)
- Nausea and vomiting
- Extreme sensitivity to light (photophobia) and sound (phonophobia)
- Aura: visual disturbances (flashes of light, blind spots, zigzag patterns), tingling sensations, speech difficulty

Triggers: Stress, hormonal changes, certain foods (caffeine, alcohol, aged cheeses, processed meats), sleep changes, weather changes, strong smells

Treatment:
- Acute/Abortive: NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen), triptans (sumatriptan, rizatriptan), ergotamines, antiemetics
- Preventive: Beta-blockers (propranolol), tricyclic antidepressants (amitriptyline), valproate, topiramate, CGRP antagonists, botulinum toxin injections

Lifestyle: Regular sleep, staying hydrated, exercise, stress management, headache diary

---

STROKE
Definition: Interruption of blood supply to part of the brain, causing cell death.

Types:
- Ischemic (87%): Blood clot blocks artery
- Hemorrhagic (13%): Blood vessel ruptures

FAST Warning Signs:
- Face drooping
- Arm weakness
- Speech difficulty
- Time to call emergency services

Additional Symptoms:
- Sudden numbness or weakness (especially one side)
- Sudden confusion or trouble understanding
- Sudden trouble seeing in one or both eyes
- Sudden severe headache with no known cause
- Sudden trouble walking, dizziness, loss of balance

Treatment:
- Ischemic: IV tPA (alteplase) within 4.5 hours, mechanical thrombectomy within 24 hours
- Hemorrhagic: Blood pressure control, surgical intervention, reversal of anticoagulation
- Secondary prevention: Antiplatelet therapy (aspirin, clopidogrel), anticoagulation for AFib, statins, hypertension control

=== GASTROINTESTINAL CONDITIONS ===

GASTROESOPHAGEAL REFLUX DISEASE (GERD)
Definition: Chronic condition where stomach acid repeatedly flows back into the esophagus.

Symptoms:
- Heartburn (burning sensation in chest)
- Regurgitation of food or sour liquid
- Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia)
- Chest pain, chronic cough, laryngitis
- Feeling of a lump in the throat

Diagnosis: Clinical history, endoscopy, pH monitoring, barium swallow

Treatment:
- Lifestyle: Avoid trigger foods (fatty/spicy foods, caffeine, alcohol, chocolate), eat smaller meals, don't lie down after eating, elevate head of bed, lose weight if overweight
- Medications: Antacids, H2 blockers (famotidine), proton pump inhibitors (omeprazole, pantoprazole)
- Surgery: Fundoplication for refractory cases

Complications: Barrett's esophagus (precancerous changes), esophageal stricture, esophageal cancer

---

IRRITABLE BOWEL SYNDROME (IBS)
Definition: Functional gastrointestinal disorder characterized by abdominal pain associated with changes in bowel habits.

Subtypes: IBS-C (constipation predominant), IBS-D (diarrhea predominant), IBS-M (mixed)

Symptoms:
- Recurring abdominal pain, at least 1 day/week in the past 3 months
- Related to defecation or associated with change in frequency or form of stool
- Bloating and gas
- Diarrhea, constipation, or alternating between both

Triggers: Stress, certain foods (FODMAPs - fermentable oligosaccharides, disaccharides, monosaccharides, polyols), hormonal changes

Treatment:
- Low FODMAP diet
- Fiber supplementation (for IBS-C)
- Antidiarrheals (loperamide) for IBS-D
- Antispasmodics (dicyclomine) for cramping
- Antidepressants for pain modulation
- Cognitive behavioral therapy, stress management

=== MUSCULOSKELETAL CONDITIONS ===

OSTEOARTHRITIS
Definition: Degenerative joint disease characterized by breakdown of cartilage and underlying bone.

Commonly Affected Joints: Knees, hips, hands, spine, feet

Symptoms:
- Joint pain that worsens with activity and improves with rest
- Stiffness, especially in the morning or after sitting (usually < 30 minutes)
- Joint swelling and tenderness
- Grating sensation or crunching sounds (crepitus)
- Loss of flexibility and range of motion
- Bone spurs (osteophytes)

Risk Factors: Age, female sex, obesity, joint injuries, repetitive stress, genetics, bone deformities

Treatment:
- Non-pharmacological: Weight loss, physical therapy, exercise (low-impact), assistive devices
- Medications: Acetaminophen, NSAIDs (topical or oral), duloxetine, corticosteroid injections, hyaluronic acid injections
- Surgery: Joint replacement (arthroplasty) for severe cases

---

RHEUMATOID ARTHRITIS (RA)
Definition: Chronic autoimmune inflammatory disease primarily affecting joints.

Symptoms:
- Tender, warm, swollen joints — typically symmetric (both sides of body)
- Joint stiffness, especially in the morning or after inactivity (> 1 hour)
- Fatigue, fever, loss of appetite
- Rheumatoid nodules (firm bumps under skin)
- Joint deformity in advanced disease

Extra-articular Manifestations: Rheumatoid nodules, lung disease, cardiovascular disease, eye inflammation, vasculitis

Diagnosis: RF (rheumatoid factor), anti-CCP antibodies, CRP, ESR, joint X-rays

Treatment:
- DMARDs (Disease-Modifying Anti-Rheumatic Drugs): Methotrexate (first-line), hydroxychloroquine, sulfasalazine, leflunomide
- Biologic DMARDs: TNF inhibitors (etanercept, adalimumab), IL-6 inhibitors (tocilizumab)
- Corticosteroids for short-term flare control
- NSAIDs for symptom relief
- Physical therapy, occupational therapy

=== MENTAL HEALTH CONDITIONS ===

DEPRESSION (Major Depressive Disorder)
Definition: A mood disorder causing persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest affecting daily functioning.

Symptoms (at least 5 for 2+ weeks, including depressed mood or anhedonia):
- Depressed mood most of the day, nearly every day
- Markedly diminished interest or pleasure in activities (anhedonia)
- Significant weight change or appetite disturbance
- Insomnia or hypersomnia
- Psychomotor agitation or retardation
- Fatigue or loss of energy
- Feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt
- Difficulty thinking, concentrating, or making decisions
- Recurrent thoughts of death or suicide

Treatment:
- Psychotherapy: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), interpersonal therapy, psychodynamic therapy
- Antidepressants: SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline, escitalopram — first-line), SNRIs (venlafaxine, duloxetine), TCAs, MAOIs
- Combination: Medication + therapy most effective
- Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) for treatment-resistant depression
- Lifestyle: Exercise, sleep hygiene, social support

---

ANXIETY DISORDERS
Types: Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic Disorder, Social Anxiety Disorder, Specific Phobias

GAD Symptoms:
- Excessive anxiety and worry about multiple events or activities (more days than not for 6+ months)
- Difficulty controlling worry
- Restlessness or feeling on edge
- Easily fatigued, difficulty concentrating
- Irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance

Panic Disorder: Recurrent unexpected panic attacks with persistent concern about future attacks or behavioral changes

Treatment:
- CBT (most effective for anxiety disorders)
- Medications: SSRIs/SNRIs (first-line), benzodiazepines (short-term), buspirone (GAD)
- Relaxation techniques, mindfulness, exposure therapy

=== INFECTIOUS DISEASES ===

URINARY TRACT INFECTION (UTI)
Definition: Infection of any part of the urinary system (kidneys, ureters, bladder, urethra).

Types:
- Cystitis (bladder infection): Most common
- Pyelonephritis (kidney infection): More serious

Symptoms of Cystitis:
- Burning sensation when urinating (dysuria)
- Frequent urge to urinate (urgency/frequency)
- Cloudy, dark, bloody, or strong-smelling urine
- Pelvic pain (in women), rectal pain (in men)

Symptoms of Pyelonephritis (additional):
- Upper back and flank pain
- High fever with chills
- Nausea and vomiting

Risk Factors: Female anatomy, sexual activity, menopause, urinary tract abnormalities, catheter use, weakened immunity

Treatment:
- Uncomplicated cystitis: 3-5 days of antibiotics (trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole, nitrofurantoin, fosfomycin)
- Pyelonephritis: 7-14 days of antibiotics (fluoroquinolones, trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole, or IV antibiotics if severe)
- Increase fluid intake

=== ENDOCRINE CONDITIONS ===

HYPOTHYROIDISM
Definition: Underactive thyroid gland producing insufficient thyroid hormone.

Symptoms:
- Fatigue and sluggishness
- Increased sensitivity to cold
- Constipation
- Pale, dry skin and hair loss
- Puffy face, brittle nails
- Slowed heart rate (bradycardia)
- Depression, impaired memory
- Muscle weakness, stiffness, aches
- Elevated cholesterol, weight gain
- In women: Heavier than normal or irregular menstrual periods

Causes: Hashimoto's thyroiditis (autoimmune), thyroid surgery, radiation therapy, certain medications (lithium, amiodarone)

Diagnosis: TSH (elevated), Free T4 (low)

Treatment: Levothyroxine (synthetic T4) — dose adjusted to normalize TSH

---

HYPERTHYROIDISM
Definition: Overactive thyroid gland producing excess thyroid hormone.

Symptoms:
- Unintentional weight loss despite increased appetite
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat (tachycardia, palpitations, atrial fibrillation)
- Nervousness, anxiety, irritability
- Tremor (especially in hands and fingers)
- Sweating and heat intolerance
- Increased frequency of bowel movements
- Goiter (enlarged thyroid gland)
- Fatigue and muscle weakness
- Difficulty sleeping
- Skin thinning, hair loss

Causes: Graves' disease (autoimmune — most common), thyroid nodules, thyroiditis

Treatment: Antithyroid drugs (methimazole, propylthiouracil), radioactive iodine therapy, thyroidectomy, beta-blockers for symptom control
